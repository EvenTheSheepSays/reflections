What happens when you initialize a repository? Why do you need to do it?

	The repo will be built with .git folder without any commits.

How is the staging area different from the working directory and the repository?
What value do you think it offers?

	Staging area is for the future commits to be made and working directory is a folder with a selected snapshot of a repo.

How can you use the staging area to make sure you have one commit per logical
change?

	By adding every changed file that affected the said logical change to it and then commiting everything.

What are some situations when branches would be helpful in keeping your history
organized? How would branches help?

	Branches are needed when something experimental should be introduced into the project but without harming the main branch. Later the branches can be merged.

How do the diagrams help you visualize the branch structure?

	It helps me to know if there are unreachable branches and also to see the whole tree.

What is the result of merging two branches together? Why do we represent it in
the diagram the way we do?

   To watch over the progress of the developement of separate branches to evade conflicts during the merging.

What are the pros and cons of Git's automatic merging vs. always doing merges
manually?

   Automatic is faster, but manual is more precise, universal and reliable.
